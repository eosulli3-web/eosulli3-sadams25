    Comparison: Global Carbon Emissions & Exchange Rates

    Can the inequality between countries be visualized?

    World Map Comparison of Country Emissions per Capita vs. Exchange Rate

    The graphs below feature each country's emission output by year, and below that their global exchange rate by year. The graphs below highlight a few trends. Firstly, the southern hemisphere, even today still puts out next to no carbon emissiosn in comparison to the northern hemisphere, where emissions have continued to rise. A correlation then arises when the global exchange rate seems to have the opposite trend. Southern countries have a much higher exchange rate, showing their poverty in relation to the U.S. dollar.

    Average Global Exchange Rate and Total Emissisons by Year

    This section allows you to see the change in average exchange value as well as total carbon emissisions over time through the use of bar graphs. The first graph actually reflects the geopolitical power of the american dollar versus the rest of the world. The dip in the bar graph in 2021 shows the hit the American economy took in it's shutdown during COVID-19, and how much of the world's choosing not to do so actually allowed them to temporarily increase their monetary value in comparison to the USD. Following with the graph, the world return to it's pre-COVID rates. The total emissisions graph actually shows not a decrease in emissions, but holes in the UN data. Despite the fact that it is a widely accepted fact carbon emissiosn are at an all time high, the data that the UN has collected is incomplete and unable to account for mucch of the carbon emission which shows up as a decrease in overall emissions.
