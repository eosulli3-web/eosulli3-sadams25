    Carbon Emissions Around the World

    The different visualizations below allow for easy visualization of trends within both specific countries as well as regions. The most southern regions are beginning to lighten, but they're emissions growth is outpaced still by many western nations. Notably, Australia serves as a general exception to this having a higher emissions per capita than many country in Europe, and this could be attributed to it's strong ties to Europe as well as it's military signifcance compared to it's relatively low population.

    Getting Specific

    The data below is still in comparison to population, so some small countries such as Aruba, are seen as having a disproportionatly high rate of carbon emissions per capita compared to others. Qatar is a consistent front runner due to their economy running on the gas and oil industry and its smaller population compared to other Middle eastern countries. Even despite the massive population, America still ranks as one of the highest per capita emission countries.
