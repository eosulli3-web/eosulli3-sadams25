    Global Total Carbon Emissions

    A look at who and how much.

    Total Emissions by Continent

    The graph below shows by continent total carbon emissions. Asia has a staggering lead on the other continents, showing just how much of the world's manufacturing is taking place in Asia. Africa and South America are close in last place, both continents still having economies based largely in agriculture. This graph stands in contrast to the per capita break down's which had many western countries leading in emissions, also demonstrating just how populated Asia is.

    Continent Emissions Broken Down by Country

    The charts below show a break down first of the world, and then by continent. China has a strong lead in emissions producing almost 20,000,000 metric tons more than America in second place. Third, India, falls even more behind producing only 12,000,000 metric tons, about 1/5 of China's overall emissions. The large gap shows just how much manufacturing power China has. In the continent specific charts below you see the balance or lack thereof in emissions output in each continent.

    China produces an unsurprising overwhelming majority of carbon pollution.

    Europe has a relatively more even spread of carbon emissions compared to the other continents with 6 different countries making up over half of emissions.

    The United States dominates in a landslide, even producing a higher percentage than China in Asia.

    While Brazil producs a majority, several other countries also have high outputs leaving the continent more balanced than North America, but less than Europe.

    Telling a similar story to South America, one nation, South Africa, produces a strong majority of carbon pollution, but several other countries hold strong portions of the total.

    Dominating the most of any country in a continent, Australia produces over 2 million more emissions than the second largest producer in the Oceania region.
