    Who's Money Has Value?

    Value and Country

    The graph below demonstrates which countries have had relatively consistent economies and those that have had very tumultuous ones. A prominent example is Iraq which sees the worst exchange value of 42k to a dollar in 2023. It is importnat to note that poor exchange value does not automatically correlate to political or social unrest; Vietnam, for example, has consistently high (poor) exchange rate and yet has had no civil or economic unrest.

    Change Over Time

    The map below shows a visualization of individual countries exchange rates over time. It easily highlights outliers such as Iraq and Somalia, but fails to encapsulate the large differences between other states, the dark blue shading ranging almost 10k. That further demonstrates the true disparity between the lighter souther regions and the rest of the world. Over time the UN was able to gain less and less data, specifically seeming to populate in Africa where there are large regions of civil and governmental unrest.
